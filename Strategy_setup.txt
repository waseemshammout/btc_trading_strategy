Option 1:
1. Moving Average Crossover (Trend Following)

Idea: Ride trends, avoid chop.

Use two moving averages (e.g., 20-period and 50-period)
Buy when short MA crosses above long MA
Sell when short MA crosses below long MA

Why it works:
Captures sustained moves in ETH, which often trends strongly.

Weakness:
Gets chopped up in sideways markets.


--------------------------------------------------------------

2. Support & Resistance Bounce

Idea: ETH often respects key levels.

Identify horizontal support/resistance zones
Buy near support
Sell near resistance

Example logic:

If price touches support + shows rejection → enter long
If price hits resistance + stalls → exit or short

Why it works:
Crypto markets are very reactive to visible levels.

Weakness:
Levels can break hard (fake confidence).

--------------------------------------------------------------

3. RSI Mean Reversion

Idea: Short-term overbought/oversold moves revert.

Use RSI (14 period)
Buy when RSI < 30
Sell when RSI > 70

Why it works:
ETH often overextends in short timeframes.

Weakness:
Fails badly in strong trends (can stay overbought/oversold).




4. Breakout Strategy

Idea: Catch volatility expansion.

Define a range (e.g., last 24h high/low)
Buy when price breaks above high
Sell when price breaks below low

Optional filter:

Only take breakout if volume increases

Why it works:
ETH frequently makes strong breakout moves.

Weakness:
Fake breakouts are common.




5. Simple VWAP Strategy (Intraday)

Idea: Institutions anchor around VWAP.

Use VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)
Buy when price is above VWAP and pulls back to it
Sell when price loses VWAP

Why it works:
Good for intraday trading.

Weakness:
Not ideal for longer timeframes.



6. Fixed % Grid (Very Simple Bot Style)

Idea: Exploit volatility without prediction.

Place buy orders every X% down (e.g., every 2%)
Place sell orders every X% up

Why it works:
ETH is volatile → lots of oscillation.

Weakness:
Can get stuck if price trends strongly in one direction.
